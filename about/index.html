Why we exist

Financial education wasn't built for people like you. So we built this instead.

Most financial education assumes you already have money, already went to business school, or already know what a treasury bill is. Robayer WealthLab exists for everyone that assumption leaves out — built for people starting with their first GH₵100, not their first GH₵100,000.

The founder

Robert Loh Kobla

Founder & CEO

Robert started Robayer WealthLab after watching too many smart, hardworking people in his own circle stay away from investing — not because they didn't want to build something, but because no one had ever explained it to them in plain language. Treasury bills, MoMo savings, the Ghana Stock Exchange — all of it felt like it belonged to people with more money, more connections, or more schooling than they had.

He didn't set out to build a financial institution. He set out to be the person he wished he'd had early on: someone slightly ahead on the journey, willing to explain the confusing parts without making anyone feel behind.

That's still the whole model. No hype, no guaranteed returns, no jargon for the sake of sounding smart — just honest, practical guidance for wherever you're starting from, delivered one guide, one article, and one better decision at a time.

Mission and vision

Mission

Why we get up in the morning

To make honest, practical financial education available to every Ghanaian — regardless of income, education, or where they're starting from.

Vision

Where we're headed

A Ghana where treasury bills, the GSE, and basic investing are as familiar to ordinary people as mobile money already is — where starting small isn't a barrier, it's just the first step.
